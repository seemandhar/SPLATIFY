3D Gaussian Splatting for Real-Time Radiance Field Rendering

Bernhard Kerbl · Georgios Kopanas · Thomas Leimkühler · George Drettakis
ACM SIGGRAPH 2023 · Inria & MPII
Abstract. Radiance field methods have achieved photo-realistic synthesis of scenes captured with multiple photos. We introduce three key elements: (1) starting from sparse SfM points, we represent the scene with 3D anisotropic Gaussians; (2) we interleave optimization with adaptive density control; (3) we develop a fast visibility-aware, tile-based rasterizer. Real-time rendering at state-of-the-art quality.
1Introduction

Radiance field methods, starting with NeRF, have achieved high-quality novel-view synthesis from a set of photos. However, volumetric MLPs are slow to train and render. We propose an explicit representation: a set of 3D Gaussians that can be projected and composited via a tile-based GPU rasterizer.

33D Gaussian Representation

We represent the scene as a set of N 3D Gaussians, each with a mean μ, a covariance matrix Σ, an opacity α, and spherical-harmonic (SH) color coefficients for view-dependent appearance:

G(x) = exp(−½(x−μ)ᵀ Σ⁻¹ (x−μ)) (1)

For optimization stability Σ is parameterized via a rotation R (quaternion) and a diagonal scale S:

Σ = R S Sᵀ Rᵀ (2)
3.1Differentiable Tile Rasterization

We project each 3D Gaussian to 2D, sort by depth inside 16×16 tiles, and composite front-to-back using α-blending identical to NeRF's volume rendering:

C(p) = Σᵢ αᵢ Tᵢ cᵢ(d),    Tᵢ = Πⱼ<ᵢ (1 − αⱼ) (3)

The rasterizer is fully differentiable and written in CUDA; gradients flow through the α-blending to position, scale, rotation, opacity, and SH coefficients.

3.2Optimization

We supervise rendered images with the photometric L1 loss combined with a D-SSIM term:

L = (1 − λ) L₁ + λ LD-SSIM,  λ = 0.2 (4)

We use Adam with exponentially-decaying learning rates on positions (1.6×10⁻⁴ → 1.6×10⁻⁶). Opacities, scales, rotations and SH coefficients have separate learning rates. Training runs 30,000 iterations.

3.3Adaptive Density Control

Every 100 iterations, we densify and prune Gaussians based on the magnitude of the view-space position gradient ∇pL:

  • Clone: small Gaussians (‖s‖ < τs) with high gradient → duplicate and nudge along gradient.
  • Split: large Gaussians with high gradient → split into two smaller Gaussians with s ← s/1.6.
  • Prune: Gaussians with opacity α < εα or world-space scale > 10% of scene extent are removed.

4Implementation & Results

We implement the rasterizer in CUDA and the optimizer in PyTorch. MipNeRF360 outdoor scenes train to 27+ PSNR in ~30 minutes on an A6000 and render at 130 FPS at 1080p.

Hyperparameters: SH degree 3, densification from step 500 to 15000, opacity reset every 3000, gradient threshold 2×10⁻⁴, λssim = 0.2.
